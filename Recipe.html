Special Pav-Bhaji Recipe

A delicious, spicy, and buttery mashed vegetable curry served with toasted pav (soft bread rolls).

Ingredients:

For the Bhaji (Curry):

3 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
1 cup cauliflower (chopped)
1/2 cup green peas
1 medium carrot (chopped)
1 large onion (finely chopped)
2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
1 capsicum (bell pepper) (finely chopped)
1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
2-3 tbsp butter (more for extra flavor)
2 tbsp oil
2 tsp pav bhaji masala
1 tsp red chili powder
1/2 tsp turmeric powder
1 tsp cumin seeds
1 tsp coriander powder
Salt to taste
1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
1 tbsp lemon juice
2 tbsp fresh coriander (chopped)

For the Pav (Bread Rolls):

8 pav (soft bread rolls)
2 tbsp butter
1/2 tsp pav bhaji masala
1 tbsp chopped coriander

For Garnishing:

1 tbsp butter
1 small onion (chopped)
Lemon wedges
Fresh coriander

Instructions:

Step 1: Cook the Vegetables

In a pot, boil potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrot in water until soft. Drain excess water and mash them roughly.

Step 2: Prepare the Bhaji

Heat oil + butter in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
Add capsicum and sauté for 2 minutes.
Add tomato puree and cook until oil starts separating.
Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, salt, and mix well.
Add mashed vegetables, mix, and mash everything together using a potato masher.
Add kasuri methi, lemon juice, and a little butter.
Mix well.
Simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 3: Toast the Pav

Heat a tawa (flat pan), add butter, pav bhaji masala, and chopped coriander.
Slice the pav in half and toast until golden brown.

Step 4: Serve & Enjoy!

Serve hot bhaji in a plate, topped with butter.
Garnish with chopped onions, coriander, and lemon wedges.
Serve with buttery toasted pav.

Pro Tips for a Special Pav Bhaji:

Use ample butter for a rich taste!
Mash the bhaji well to get the authentic Mumbai street-style texture.
For extra flavor, add grated cheese on top!
You can add beetroot for a bright red color like restaurant pav bhaji!
